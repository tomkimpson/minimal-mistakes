Tom Kimpson

I am a theoretical physicist undertaking research for my PhD within the Astrophysics Group, at the Mullard Space Science Laboratory part of University College London. My PhD advisors are Kinwah Wu and Silvia Zane

I am currently based at Perimeter Institute for Theoretical Physics as a Visiting Graduate Fellow.

Prior to my PhD From 2011 to 2017, I worked at Intel, as part of Intel RealSense. I primarily designed computer vision algorithms for efficient hardware ASICs, including the Intel RealSense R200 and D400 RGB-D sensors. Additionally, I worked on software APIs, active illumination systems, human-computer interaction devices, and helped develop demos for trade shows, including CES 2012-2016.

I have an MS in Computer Science (AI focus) from Stanford University, where I was a research assistant for Silvio Savarese and a teaching assistant for Fei-Fei Li (CS131 & CS231N). I have a BS in EECS from UC Berkeley, where I worked in Kris Pister's lab.

Code

Throughout my research I have written a number of scientific codes, typically relating to time-frequency modelling and signal analysis of non-linear, dynamical astrophysical systems ( e.g. Pulsar - Extreme Mass Ratio Binaries). In order to enable [reproducible research](http://reproducibleresearch.net/) I am attempting to make all codes associated with published papers publicly available with documentation. This endeavour is currently a work in progress!
